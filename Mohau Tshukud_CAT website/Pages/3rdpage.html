MY LIFE DURING LOCKDOWN.


Cooking


When your uncle is a chef you're bound to know a few more recipes than boiling eggs and instant noodles. During the lockdown, my cousin who lived with my uncle started to cook some exciting stuff. Our family is pretty involved so we all joined in. Ingredients were limited so we had to work with what we had at the time. We decided to go traditional with:

  • Amagwinya/vetkoeks
  • Pizza

We went to it and all went well with the vetkoeks but when it came to the pizza I finally came to realise how important oven mitts were. The following contains a simplified (not accurate at all and based on what temperature the oven felt at the time) estimate of the types of burns and their relevant information:

Burn Temperature Signs
1st Degree 50 Degrees Celcius Redness
2nd Degree 100 Degrees Celcius Blisters
3rd Degree 200 Degrees Celcius Crust

While removing the pan of pizza out of the oven, I didn't use oven mitts but rather a huge pan thing that is used in resturants. The thing got stuck and I had to take it out but I got what felt like a 3rd degree burn on my index finger.

After all this craziness I learned some very valuable lessons:.

  1. Do not! double everything on a recipe in hopes of doubling the amount of food
  2. Oven mitts aren't necessary Oven mitts are there for a reason
