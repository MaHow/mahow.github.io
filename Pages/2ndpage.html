MY LIFE DURING LOCKDOWN.


Spraining My Leg


If you grew up in a South African household then you probably love or hate dancing. I for one love dancing but a problem arises when you can't go outside to film some wild TikToks. It was a beautiful Friday evening inside our lovely house and I was busting out some moves for our TikToks. I then started dancing in front of the TV because I was in a good mood. My cousin was watching and I wanted to get on her nerves on purpose. She wasn't budging, through years of ignoring me she has mastered it, so I had to get creative. I threw a sock at her- then another, I picked them back up and repeated. After the third throw, turns out she hadn't mastered it, after all, she flipped and gathered all her strength to throw a shoe at me. Not just any shoe- some hard and thick Fenty. I ran like I never have- unfortunately it's the lockdown and the kitchen wall was an obstacle I could not dodge. Trying to dodge the wall, in slow motion, I heard the majestic sound of ripping flesh rush through my nervous system. The pain took a minute to settle in after the swelling. Long story short the following steps for dealing with a sprained ankle actually work:

  1. Rest
  2. Use ice
  3. Compress
  4. Elevate
Fenty are a collaboration between Puma and Rihanna. Designer shoes. They have extremly thick soles and are very hard.
